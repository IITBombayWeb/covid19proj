Requirements for a 10-Bed ICU

This page provides the requirements for a 10-bed ICU that can cater to COVID-19 patients requiring mechanical ventilation support. They are classified under the heads of:

  • Staffing
  • Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)
  • Infrastructure
  • Equipment
  • Drugs
  • Expendables
  • Oxygen related items
  • Linen
  • Pantry and Miscellaneous

Infrastructure

Item Measure Remarks
Large Room 20 m X 11 m 225 sqft space required for 1 patient in open ICU, 250 sq feet if ICU has a cubicle format. 10 patients need 2250 sqft area (209 sq mts).
Airconditioning Hospital requirement of airconditioning is 1 ton AC for every 150-250 sqft. So for an area of 2550 sft we need about 10 tonne airconditioner, with at least 12 air-changes per hour.

Oxygen related items

Item Measure Remarks

Oxygen cylinder trolley 2 For 6000 lit cylinder for shifting of cylinders to manifold room
Oxygen cylinder 1246 liters 10 reserve /backup
Oxygen cylinder trolley 2 For 1246 lit cylinder, 10 full cylinders per day (only to be used for patient transport and as backup oxygen in case of oxygen pipeline failure)
Cylinder opening key 2+2 2 for Manifold room and 2 for ICU
Bull nose fitting with flowmeter 10
Adjustable spanner 2+2 For fixing bull nose fitting, 2 for Manifold room and 2 for ICU
The Oxygen requirement has been calculated for the worst case scenario when all 10 patients are on ventilator and requiring high FiO2. The actual requirement may be less than this if only 50% patients are on ventilator and remaining are on oxygen support only.
