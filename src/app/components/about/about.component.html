About this site

This web application provides a four-week projected requirement for various medical inventory across districts, states, and the national level. This should be helpful in planning for infrastructure, arranging essential human resources and procurement of materials. MSMEs and other industries working in the production and supply chain of these essentials may use these projections to support their local government administration.

Statistical Model of COVID-19 Growth

The district-wise projections for COVID-19 carrier patients comes from a model mesoscalelab.github.io/covid19 , developed by Dr. Santosh Ansumali (Engineering Mechanics Unit, JNCASR , Bengaluru) and Dr. Aloke Kumar (Mechanical Engineering, IISc , Bengaluru). A manuscript of this work (in medRxiv) is currently under review. Some salient features of this model are:

  • It uses the deceased count, which is more reliable than the reported confirmed positive cases.

  • It is a data-driven model that uses the published data for the progression of COVID-19 related deaths world-wide. The commonalities from this data is then applied it to district level data to obtain projections of the progression.

  • The projections are adaptive, continuously correcting, based on the currently available data. See the FAQ section for a graph and explanation.

Indian Patient Data

This model uses the patient database from covid19india.org, which is a crowd-sourced (sanitised and self-correcting) public data base of reported COVID-19 positive cases in the country.

Inventory Data

The model for the projected inventory requirement (PDF link here) was developed by Dr. Deepak Padmanabhan (MD, DM, Cardiologist, Sri Jayadeva Institute of Cardiology, Bengaluru) and Prof. P Sunthar (Chemical Engineering, IIT Bombay, Mumbai). This is based on the various levels of severity of the disease and the type of care and equipment each case needs. See details in the PDF document.
