Frequently Asked Questions

  1. How do I rely on the accuracy of the predictions?

    1. As with any model with uncertainties, here too we cannot assure exactness of the prediction. We are continuously improving the predictions as we get fresh data. Our model correctly captures the generic behaviour seen across the world for COVID-19. We have also provided two scenarios “Moderate and Worst case”. It is better to be well stocked beforehand than to be without any clue of the future requirements.

  2. I think the inventory estimate for item XYZ is wrong or I want to see the estimate of item ABC. Whom to contact?

    1. Please provide some justifications, along with your professional experience and affiliation in an email to p.sunthar at iitb . ac . in

  3. Why is the current date older than today's date?

    1. The prediction for infected cases is currently based on a fixed initial date (which will be advanced at regular intervals). When the model is improved, the "current" (initial) date will become closer to the actual present date.

  4. The data for my district is not there. When can I get the numbers?

    1. The model for the prediction requires that the district to have a significant number of positive cases detected. Once we have that data, we will update this site as well.

  5. I know that my district has more than N positive cases. Still, there are no projections, why?

    1. Please write to ansumali at jncasr . ac . in with the details of the number and the dates they were discovered. We will update the predictions after verification from reliable sources

  6. How do I obtain district level predictions

    1. Use the India map on the left to zoom into the state and district.

    2. Hover the mouse on the district to see the name.

    3. Click on the chosen district.

    4. The data for the inventory will appear on the right.

    5. On the top, choose the Week in future to get the projected numbers for that week.

  7. How do I obtain a state and India level data?

    1. Click on any district of the state.

    2. The data for that district and the state and national level will be displayed in the inventory table.
